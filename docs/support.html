FAQ

How does the Pomodoro Technique work?

›

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that uses a timer to break work into intervals (typically 25 minutes) separated by short breaks. Immerse Flow helps you customize these intervals to match your workflow.

What is the difference between Short Break and Long Break?

›

Short Breaks are quick 5-minute rests between focus sessions. Long Breaks are longer 15-minute breaks that occur after a set number of focus sessions (configurable in Settings → Focus Behavior → Long Break After).

How do I customize timer durations?

›

Open Settings → Focus Behavior. You can customize Focus Mode duration, Short Break, Long Break, and set Duration Step to control time increments (1 min or 5 min intervals).

What does "Keep Screen Awake" do?

›

When enabled, Immerse Flow prevents your device from going to sleep during an active focus session. This keeps the timer visible on your screen.

How do Activities work?

›

Activities let you categorize your focus sessions (e.g., "Coding", "Writing", "Study"). Create activities in Settings → Activities, then select one before starting a timer. Your statistics will track time spent on each activity.

Where is my data stored?

›

All your focus sessions, statistics, and settings are stored locally on your device in Immerse Flow's private storage. Your data never leaves your device and is not uploaded to any server. See our Privacy Policy for more details.

How do I change the app theme?

›

Open Settings → Appearance → Background. Choose from gradient themes like Blush, Sky, Peach, Matcha, Lavender, Milk, Twilight, or photo backgrounds like Sunset, Mountains, Forest, and Aurora.

What are the different timer display styles?

›

Immerse Flow offers 5 timer styles in Settings → Appearance → Timer Display:

  • Lap - Inner circle with gradient progress
  • Pie - Wedge-style countdown
  • Ring - Classic circular ring
  • Digits - Large digital countdown
  • Bubble - Floating bubble design

How do I enable notifications?

›

Open Settings → General & Support → Notifications and toggle it on. If this is your first time, iOS will ask for permission. If you previously denied permission, the toggle will open iOS Settings where you can enable notifications for Immerse Flow.
